What I Enjoy Learning and Building

I’ve always liked building things. When I was younger that mostly meant spending hours building Lego sets, but what I really enjoyed was taking them apart and trying to build something new from the same pieces. That curiosity eventually turned into robotics. In middle school I started working with Lego robotics kits where we programmed small robots that used simple infrared sensors to detect walls or obstacles. At the time the systems were pretty basic, but it was the first time I saw how hardware and software could come together to make something actually move and respond to its environment. As I got older that interest grew into building more real robots in early high school, where the projects required more thoughtful design, wiring, and programming instead of just assembling a kit. That progression from physically building machines to writing the code that controlled them naturally led me toward computer science. In college I’ve found that the same mindset of experimenting, iterating, and improving systems applies directly to software. One area that I’ve become especially interested in is machine learning. I enjoy training models, working with large datasets, and testing different approaches to see what signals can be uncovered. That interest becomes even more exciting when it’s applied to markets and financial data, where the patterns are complex and honestly just really interesting to see.
